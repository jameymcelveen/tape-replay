Honesty by design

TapeReplay is pessimistic on purpose. It measures whether a strategy survives after costs on data you did not tune against.

Core rules

  1. One green day is not evidence. Exploratory runs are clearly labeled and should never be marketed as proof.
  2. Freeze before you evaluate. Commit locks strategy parameters. Evaluate uses that frozen config on fresh dates.
  3. Costs are on by default. Slippage and fees reduce reported P&L — headline numbers are net, not fantasy gross.
  4. No look-ahead. Entry signals use only information available at bar open (running daily high, not the final daily high).

Chart backtest vs Strategy lab

Chart backtest shows intraday ORB/PMH against a perfect-hindsight ceiling — great for intuition, not a substitute for commit/evaluate.

Strategy lab is the proper train/test workflow for the daily-high-breakout DSL engine.

Healthy workflow: exploratory → commit in-sample → evaluate out-of-sample → only then decide if the strategy deserves more data or live scrutiny.
