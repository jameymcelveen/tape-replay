Chart backtest

Run intraday rules (ORB or PMH) against stored minute bars, render candlesticks with volume, and overlay strategy entries/exits plus a perfect-hindsight benchmark.

Opening the view

Click Chart backtest in the app header. The layout expands to full width for the chart.

Controls

FieldDescription
Ticker / DateAny symbol with minute bars in your local SQLite database.
From / To (ET)Intraday window in US Eastern time. Use presets for RTH or full session.
RuleORB — opening-range breakout. PMH — premarket high breakout.
OR minutesLength of opening range for ORB (default 5).
Stop % / Target %Hard stop and profit target from entry. Same-bar stop wins if both hit.
ScopeRegular — RTH only. All — pre + RTH + post (until 8pm ET).

Reading the chart

  • ▲ Azure arrow — strategy entry (Buy).
  • ▼ Amber arrow — strategy exit (label shows stop, target, or close).
  • ● Violet circle — perfect hindsight buy/sell (ceiling benchmark).
  • Volume bars are tinted: violet = premarket, slate = regular, sky = post-market.
  • Time axis is labeled in US/Eastern even though bars are stored in UTC.
Sparse minutes: only minutes that actually traded are plotted. Missing minutes are not synthesized — markers snap to the nearest real bar within one minute.

Capture percent

capturePct = strategy P&L per share ÷ hindsight profit per share × 100. A negative capture means the rule lost money while a large hindsight move was available (e.g. stopped out of an ORB while the stock later ran).

Example: VSME 2026-06-16

With full-session scope and 5-minute ORB:

  • ORB entry near $2.45, quick stop out.
  • Hindsight ceiling roughly $2.01 → $2.90 (premarket low to afternoon high).
  • The chart makes the gap between your rule and the ceiling obvious.

Use RTH preset if you want hindsight computed on regular session only.
